Answers to the problems we hear most.

Real questions from homeowners dealing with water, fire, mold, and biohazard damage.

My pipe burst and there's water everywhere — what do I do first?

Shut off your water at the main valve if you can, turn off electricity to affected areas, and move valuables out of standing water. Then call us — fast extraction in the first 24–48 hours is what prevents the bigger problems like warped floors, ruined drywall, and mold growth.

The water dried on its own. Do I still need to do anything?

Usually yes. Surfaces can look dry while moisture stays trapped inside walls, subfloors, and insulation. That hidden moisture is the most common cause of mold appearing weeks later. We use moisture meters to check what's still wet behind the surface, not just what you can see.

I found mold in a closet/bathroom — can I just clean it with bleach?

Surface bleaching often hides the problem without solving it, because mold roots into porous materials and keeps growing if the moisture source is still there. Proper remediation means containing the area, removing affected material safely, and fixing the moisture cause so it doesn't come back.

The fire is out but everything smells like smoke. Will that go away?

Smoke odor doesn't fade on its own — soot embeds into walls, ceilings, HVAC systems, and belongings. It needs targeted cleaning and deodorization to actually remove, not mask. We focus on removing the source of the odor along with the visible soot damage.

Is biohazard cleanup something I can handle myself?

We strongly recommend not attempting it. Biohazard and trauma situations involve health risks and require proper protective equipment, safe handling, and disposal procedures. We handle these with discretion, sensitivity, and safety-oriented processes so you don't have to.

Do you document everything for my insurance claim?

Yes. We focus on clear documentation throughout — photos of affected areas, scope notes, and organized communication — so you and your insurance contact have what you need without the back-and-forth.
